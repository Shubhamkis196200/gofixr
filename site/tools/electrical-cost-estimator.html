Electrical Cost Guide

Average Electrician Rates (2026)

  • Hourly rate: $80-150/hour
  • Service call: $75-150
  • Emergency rate: $150-300/hour
  • Typical minimum: 1-2 hours

Common Projects & Costs

  • Outlet/switch replacement: $100-200
  • GFCI outlet: $150-300
  • Ceiling fan install: $200-500
  • Light fixture: $200-400
  • Recessed lights (each): $200-400
  • New circuit: $300-800
  • Panel upgrade: $1,500-3,500
  • EV charger circuit: $400-1,200

⚠️ NEVER DIY These

  • Electrical panel work
  • Service entrance upgrades
  • Any work requiring permits
  • Aluminum wiring remediation
  • New circuit installations
  • Work near water (bathrooms/kitchens)

When to Call an Electrician

  • Frequently tripping breakers
  • Flickering lights
  • Burning smell near outlets/switches
  • Outlets/switches warm to touch
  • Any sparking or buzzing
  • GFCI won't reset
  • Upgrading to 3-prong outlets

Electrical Safety Tips

  • Never work on live circuits
  • Test outlets before touching
  • Use GFCI outlets near water
  • Don't overload outlets
  • Replace damaged cords immediately
  • Keep fire extinguisher accessible

Permits & Codes

Most electrical work requires permits and inspections. Unpermitted work can:

  • Void homeowner's insurance
  • Cause problems when selling
  • Create safety hazards
  • Require expensive corrections later
