HVAC Sizing Guide

Why Size Matters

Properly sized HVAC systems are more efficient, last longer, and maintain better comfort. Too small won't cool/heat adequately; too large cycles on/off frequently, wasting energy.

BTU and Tonnage

  • BTU: British Thermal Unit - heat energy needed
  • Ton: 12,000 BTU per hour
  • Rule of thumb: 20-30 BTU per sq ft for AC

Typical System Sizes

  • 1.5 tons (18,000 BTU): 600-900 sq ft
  • 2 tons (24,000 BTU): 900-1,200 sq ft
  • 2.5 tons (30,000 BTU): 1,200-1,500 sq ft
  • 3 tons (36,000 BTU): 1,500-1,800 sq ft
  • 3.5 tons (42,000 BTU): 1,800-2,100 sq ft
  • 4 tons (48,000 BTU): 2,100-2,400 sq ft
  • 5 tons (60,000 BTU): 2,400-3,000 sq ft

HVAC System Costs (2026)

  • 2-ton system: $3,500-5,500
  • 3-ton system: $4,000-6,500
  • 4-ton system: $4,500-7,500
  • 5-ton system: $5,500-9,000

Includes equipment and installation. High-efficiency or multi-zone systems cost more.

Energy Efficiency Ratings

  • SEER: 14-16 (standard), 18-20+ (high-efficiency)
  • AFUE (furnace): 80% (standard), 90-98% (high-efficiency)
  • HSPF (heat pump): 8-10 (standard), 10+ (high-efficiency)

Get a Professional Assessment

This calculator provides estimates. For accurate sizing, hire an HVAC pro to perform a Manual J load calculation. Factors include:

  • Local climate data
  • Home construction details
  • Window types and placement
  • Ductwork condition
  • Air leakage
