How to Replace a Garbage Disposal

‚è±Ô∏è 2 hours üí∞ $100-300

What You Will Need

  • New garbage disposal unit (1/2 HP for light use, 3/4 HP for families)
  • Plumber's wrench or disposal wrench (Allen wrench)
  • Flathead and Phillips screwdrivers
  • Plumber's putty
  • Wire nuts and electrical tape
  • Bucket and towels

Step 1: Disconnect Power

Safety first ‚Äî turn off the circuit breaker that powers the disposal. Verify it's off by trying to turn on the disposal. If your unit is plug-in, simply unplug it. For hardwired units, you'll disconnect the wiring in Step 5. Never work on a disposal with the power on.

Step 2: Remove the Old Disposal

Place a bucket under the disposal to catch water. Disconnect the discharge tube (the pipe on the side going to the drain trap) by loosening the slip nut. If a dishwasher drain hose is connected, pull it off. Support the disposal with one hand, then insert an Allen wrench into the bottom mount and twist counterclockwise ‚Äî or rotate the lower mounting ring counterclockwise until it drops free. Set the old unit aside.

Step 3: Install the Mounting Assembly

If the new unit uses the same Quick Lock or EZ Mount system as the old one, you can keep the existing flange and mounting hardware. Otherwise, push the old flange up from below, scrape off the old plumber's putty, and remove the snap ring, backup flange, and mounting ring. Roll fresh plumber's putty into a 1/2-inch rope, press it around the drain opening, and seat the new flange. From below, slide on the backup flange, fiber gasket, and mounting ring. Install the snap ring and tighten the three mounting screws evenly until the flange is snug against the sink.

Step 4: Wire the New Disposal

Remove the electrical cover plate on the bottom of the new unit. Feed the electrical cable through the clamp. Connect black to black, white to white using wire nuts. Attach the green or bare copper wire to the green ground screw. Tuck the wires in and replace the cover plate. If your disposal is the plug-in type, attach the power cord to these same connections.

Step 5: Mount the Unit and Connect Plumbing

Lift the disposal up to the mounting ring and rotate it clockwise until it locks into place ‚Äî you'll feel it click. Connect the discharge tube to the side outlet using the gasket and flange provided. Reconnect the P-trap. If you have a dishwasher, use a screwdriver to knock out the dishwasher inlet plug from inside the disposal, then connect the dishwasher hose with a hose clamp.

Step 6: Test Everything

Turn the breaker back on. Run cold water and flip the disposal switch. Let it run for 30 seconds. Check every connection point underneath for leaks ‚Äî the mounting flange, discharge tube, and P-trap joints. Tighten any dripping fittings. If the unit hums but doesn't spin, turn it off and use the Allen wrench on the bottom to manually rotate the grinding plate to clear any obstruction.

üí° Pro Tip

Always run cold water (not hot) when using your disposal ‚Äî cold water solidifies grease so the blades can chop it up, while hot water melts it and causes pipe clogs downstream. Avoid putting fibrous foods like celery, artichokes, or corn husks in the disposal.
