How to Patch Drywall Holes

What You Will Need

  • Spackle or joint compound (lightweight for small holes)
  • Drywall patch kit or mesh tape (for holes over 2 inches)
  • Putty knife (4-inch and 6-inch)
  • Sandpaper (120 and 220 grit)
  • Primer and matching paint

Small Holes (Nail/Screw Holes)

Apply a small amount of lightweight spackle with your finger or a putty knife. Smooth it flush with the wall. Let it dry 30 minutes, then sand lightly with 220-grit paper. One coat is usually enough for holes under ¬º inch.

Medium Holes (1-4 inches)

Apply a self-adhesive mesh patch over the hole. Spread joint compound over the patch with a 6-inch putty knife, extending 2-3 inches beyond the edges. Let dry completely (4-6 hours), then apply a second thin coat. Sand smooth when dry.

Large Holes (Over 4 inches)

Cut a clean rectangle around the damage using a drywall saw. Cut a new piece of drywall to fit. Secure backing strips (furring strips or drywall clips) behind the opening, then screw the patch piece in place. Tape seams with paper tape and apply 3 coats of joint compound, feathering wider each time. Sand between coats.

Finishing

Always prime the repaired area before painting ‚Äî spackle and joint compound absorb paint differently than the surrounding wall, creating visible spots called "flashing." Use a matching roller nap (smooth for smooth walls, thick for textured).

For textured walls (knockdown, orange peel), buy a can of spray texture from the hardware store. Practice on cardboard first to match the existing pattern before spraying the repair area.
