Water heater replacement involves gas lines, electrical wiring, and heavy lifting. Many jurisdictions require a permit and inspection. If you're not experienced with plumbing and gas/electrical work, hire a licensed plumber. Improper installation can cause gas leaks, carbon monoxide poisoning, flooding, or fire.

What You Will Need

  • New water heater (match the fuel type ‚Äî gas or electric ‚Äî and capacity of the old one)
  • Flexible water heater connectors (stainless braided)
  • T&P (temperature and pressure) relief valve and discharge pipe
  • Gas flex connector and Teflon tape (gas units) or electrical wire and breaker (electric)
  • Pipe wrench, adjustable wrench, tubing cutter
  • Garden hose for draining
  • Hand truck ‚Äî water heaters weigh 120-160 lbs empty
  • Drip pan for under the new unit

Step 1: Shut Off Energy and Water

For a gas water heater, turn the gas control knob to "OFF" and close the gas shut-off valve on the supply line (the valve handle should be perpendicular to the pipe). For an electric water heater, turn off the dedicated 240V breaker at the panel ‚Äî verify with a voltage tester. Then close the cold water inlet valve on top of the heater.

Step 2: Drain the Tank

Connect a garden hose to the drain valve near the bottom of the tank. Route the hose to a floor drain, sump pit, or outside (downhill from the heater). Open the drain valve (use a flathead screwdriver if it's a slotted type). Open a hot water faucet on an upper floor to let air into the system ‚Äî this breaks the vacuum and allows draining. A 40-50 gallon tank takes 20-45 minutes to drain completely. Caution: this water is scalding hot if the heater was recently running.

Step 3: Disconnect Everything

Disconnect the cold and hot water lines ‚Äî if they're copper, you may need to cut them with a tubing cutter. Disconnect the gas line (gas unit) or electrical conduit (electric unit). Remove the vent pipe from the draft hood (gas). Disconnect the T&P discharge pipe. Use a hand truck to wheel the old heater out. A full-size water heater won't fit through a 24" doorway easily ‚Äî measure first.

Step 4: Position the New Unit

Place a drip pan under the new location (required by code in many areas). Set the new heater in the pan. Ensure it's level ‚Äî use shims if needed. Gas water heaters require specific clearances: typically 12" from the front for combustion air and 1" from walls on the sides. The unit should be elevated 18" off the ground in garages (FVIR-compliant models excepted) to keep the flame above potential gas fumes.

Step 5: Connect Plumbing and Energy

Install new flexible stainless-steel water connectors ‚Äî they're far easier and more reliable than rigid pipe. Wrap pipe threads with Teflon tape (6-8 wraps clockwise). For gas units: install a new gas flex connector (never reuse the old one), apply pipe dope to threaded joints, and connect the vent pipe to the draft hood and then to the flue. For electric units: connect the wiring through the junction box on the heater ‚Äî black to black, white to white, ground to ground. Install the T&P relief valve in the designated port and run the discharge pipe downward to within 6 inches of the floor ‚Äî never cap or plug this pipe.

Step 6: Fill, Test, and Commission

Open the cold water supply valve. Open a hot faucet and wait for a steady stream (this means the tank is full and air is purged). Check every connection for leaks. For gas: apply soapy water to all gas joints ‚Äî bubbles mean a leak. Turn on the gas valve, set the thermostat to 120¬∞F, and light the pilot per the manufacturer's instructions. For electric: with the tank full, turn on the breaker ‚Äî never energize an empty electric water heater as it will burn out the heating elements instantly. Check for hot water at a faucet within 30-60 minutes.
