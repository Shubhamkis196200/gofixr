How to Caulk a Bathtub

What You Will Need

  • Kitchen/bath silicone caulk (100% silicone, mold-resistant)
  • Caulk gun
  • Utility knife or caulk removal tool
  • Rubbing alcohol or caulk remover
  • Painters tape
  • Paper towels and a spray bottle with soapy water

Step 1: Remove Old Caulk

Use a utility knife or oscillating tool to cut and peel away all old caulk. Apply caulk remover gel to stubborn spots and wait 2-3 hours. Scrub the joint clean with rubbing alcohol. The surface must be completely clean and dry ‚Äî new caulk will not bond to old caulk or soap residue.

Step 2: Tape the Edges

Apply painters tape along both sides of the joint, leaving a gap the width of your desired caulk line (typically ¬º inch). This creates clean, professional edges. Fill the tub with water before caulking ‚Äî the weight opens the joint to its widest point, so the caulk will not crack when you use the tub.

Step 3: Apply the Caulk

Cut the caulk tube tip at a 45-degree angle, making a small opening (you can always cut more). Apply steady, even pressure while moving the gun along the joint at a consistent speed. Work in one continuous line per side. Push the caulk into the gap rather than dragging it over the surface.

Step 4: Smooth and Finish

Spray the bead lightly with soapy water, then run a wet finger or caulk finishing tool along the joint in one smooth motion. Remove the painters tape immediately while the caulk is still wet ‚Äî pull at a 45-degree angle away from the caulk line. Do not use the tub for 24 hours.

üí° Pro Tip

Choose 100% silicone caulk, not "siliconized latex." Pure silicone lasts 10+ years in wet areas versus 2-3 years for latex blends. It is harder to apply but far more durable and waterproof.
