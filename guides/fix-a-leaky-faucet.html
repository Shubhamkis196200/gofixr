How to Fix a Leaky Faucet

‚è±Ô∏è 30 min üí∞ $10-30

What You Will Need

  • Adjustable wrench and Allen wrench set
  • Replacement cartridge, O-rings, or washers (match your faucet brand)
  • Plumber grease (silicone-based)
  • Flathead and Phillips screwdrivers
  • Towel and small container for parts

Step 1: Turn Off the Water

Locate the shut-off valves under the sink and turn them clockwise until fully closed. Open the faucet to release remaining pressure and drain residual water. If there are no shut-off valves under the sink, turn off the main water supply.

Step 2: Identify Your Faucet Type

Ball faucets (single handle, moves in all directions) use a ball assembly. Cartridge faucets (single or double handle, moves up/down) use a replaceable cartridge. Compression faucets (two handles, must be tightened to close) use rubber washers. Ceramic disc faucets (single handle, very smooth) use ceramic discs.

Step 3: Disassemble the Handle

Pry off the decorative cap (if any) with a flathead screwdriver. Remove the handle screw (usually Phillips or Allen). Pull the handle straight up. Take a photo at each step so you remember how to reassemble.

Step 4: Replace the Worn Part

For cartridge faucets, pull out the old cartridge and take it to the hardware store for an exact match. For compression faucets, replace the rubber washer at the bottom of the stem. Apply plumber grease to all O-rings before reassembly.

Step 5: Reassemble and Test

Reverse the disassembly steps. Hand-tighten first, then snug with a wrench ‚Äî do not over-tighten. Turn the water back on slowly and check for leaks at every connection point.

üí° Pro Tip

A faucet dripping once per second wastes over 3,000 gallons per year. Fixing it promptly saves roughly $20-30/year on your water bill and prevents mineral staining in the sink.
